DH at UNL

The Digital Humanities Graduate Studies program identifies UNL as “an international leader in the field, with over a dozen faculty from the areas of Anthropology, Art and Art History, Classics and Religious Studies, English, History, Modern Languages, and the University Libraries.” Not only is the DH program at UNL a prestigious program, but we have on-going research in each of these fields. We can credit Dr. Matthew Jockers, now Associate Dean for Research and Global Development for that. He came from Stanford University to establish a program here at Nebraska. Since his arrival, our digital humanities research projects have multiplied.

We have three major facilities for DH research here on campus. The first would be the Center for Digital Research in the Humanities, located on the third floor of Love Library South. The CDRH, as it is commonly called, "promotes collaborative, transdisciplinary digital humanities research and encourages grants in the humanities. Through its endowment, the Center provides mentoring opportunities for the next generation of humanities scholars." The CDRH collaborates not only within the University, but also with other institutions on various projects pertaining to DH research. Some of its most popular projects include the Austen Said project, the Civil War Washington Archive, the Journals of the Lewis and Clark Expedition project, and the O Say Can You See project. You can find a full list of their project publications here.

The second research facility is the Walt Whitman Archive, located on the third floor of Andrews Hall. The Whitman Archive “endeavors to make Whitman's vast work freely and conveniently accessible to scholars, students, and general readers.” It essentially houses all of Whitman’s correspondences, interviews and reminiscences, periodicals, and poetry. Created by UNL’s Dr. Ken Price and Dr. Ed Folsom of the University of Iowa, the Whitman Archive is an on-going research project that works with digital photo scans, transcriptions, encoding of documents, and aspects of literary analysis.

The Willa Cather Archive, the third major research facility, is also located on the third floor of Andrews Hall. The Cather Archive “is an ambitious endeavor to create a rich, useful, and widely-accessible site for the study of Willa Cather's life and writings. To that end, we are providing digital editions of Cather texts and scholarship free to the public as well as creating a large amount of unique, born-digital scholarly content.”. The Cather Archive functions very similarly to the Whitman Archive, collecting Cather’s novels, short fiction, interviews and speeches, journalism, and correspondences. The project is directed by UNL professor Andrew Jewell.

As we are a research-based institution, UNL has several different faculty personnel currently doing DH research. You can find a list of all participating faculty here.
