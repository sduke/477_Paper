How to Get Involved

There are several ways for undergraduate students to get involved in digital humanities research here at the University of Nebraska. We have various work and course experiences that not only introduce you to the field, but help you engage in some of the projects currently being developed at the University. The first step is knowing where to look.

One of the most obvious ways to get involved is by declaring the Undergraduate Minor in Digital Humanities. The total coursework is 18 hours, and cover a variety of experiences that introduce undergraduates to the realm of DH practices. Essentially, there are two required courses, two core electives, and two various electives, each ranging in a variety of different fields, such as English, History, Anthropology, Classics, and Theatre. Each class offers a unique perspective and experience in regards to DH. Some are very technically based, while others are very theoretically based. Both experiences are necessary for that well-rounded DH education.

Within the English major, we have the option to specialize in a specific English-related field. The Department offers a concentration in DH. Any classes in the DH field (usually from the undergraduate minor) at the 300 level and above count toward the concentration. Students can choose any DH classes that interest them: they can focus on text analysis, archives, 3-D modeling, or a combination of all of them. The concentration is essentially an abbreviated take on the overall undergraduate minor.

There is, however, an option to explore the field of DH prior to committing to a minor or concentration. We suggest taking one or both of two courses: Being Human in a Digital Age (ENGL 277) and Introduction to Digital Humanities (ENGL 278). The 277 course offers a more theoretical, humanities approach to DH and how technology influences our lives. The 278 course offers a more technical, hands-on approach to DH and what basic technological aspects are used in DH projects and research. Both classes offer a well-rounded picture of what DH is from two different viewpoints.

Looking to get more involved? Consider applying for an internship or a UCARE position at any of the various DH research centers! The CDRH is willing to hire on students to do various works on various projects. As there are so many different facets of DH, and so many different projects going on, you are sure to find something you are interested in at your skill level. The Walt Whitman and Willa Cather Archives are also willing to take on UCARE students to work with them for either the academic year or the summer. Projects vary from typical desk work, to individual research projects. See a sample UCARE proposal here.
