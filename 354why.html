Why Get Involved?

Digital humanities allows for more in-depth, alternate perspective research in humanities fields. We are now in the 21st century: everything we do, say, act, learn, is often influenced by some sort of digital revolution or technology. By participating in DH research and projects, we are ensuring that there is a future for humanities fields themselves.

Many articles are claiming that we are starting to see the death of the humanities. They argue that math and science education will ensure a more solid future for children, as well as continue to be relevant in future society. What many often forget to consider, when looking at DH, is that computer science is a major aspect of DH research. DH involves encoding, scanning, mapping, formatting, and database development. And that is just the digital side of things. The humanities side goes further into reading and understanding a text, or series of texts, or the like, as well as attempting to look at the corpus from several different potential viewpoints.

DH studies are still relevant because they help bring humanities research into the 21st century. They use technology to help bring a broader, more complete understanding of various aspects of humanities research. Not only that, but DH projects are also fun. You engage in so many new, exciting, different aspects of language and research that help you understand subjects you are interested in more completely.
